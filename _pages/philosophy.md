# Research Philosophy

## Human-Centered AI for Learning

I am interested in how AI systems can support learning without reducing learners to passive users of automated tools. My research starts from educational problems: how learners build confidence, sustain engagement, receive feedback, and develop agency when interacting with AI tutors, virtual influencers, writing assistants, and other intelligent systems.

## Methods

I combine educational theory, behavioral experiment design, survey methods, user-centered evaluation, and data analysis. I care about both measurable learning outcomes and the experience of interaction: trust, motivation, cognitive load, emotional support, and the ways learners adapt their strategies when AI becomes part of the learning environment.

## Direction

My long-term goal is to design and evaluate AI learning systems that are technically credible, pedagogically grounded, and useful in real educational contexts. I hope to contribute to human-AI interaction research by studying how learners actually use AI support, where these systems fail, and how interface design and feedback design can make them more trustworthy and effective.
